# TurboPrep — Victorian statewide launch plan

The strategy to take TurboPrep from one lighthouse team (Caulfield Grammar HPR) to every HPR-racing school in Victoria within 18 months, with sustainable engagement and a defensible revenue path.

Audience for this document: founder, anyone pitching the app to a school sport director or HPR body, and the launch lead running rollout operations.

---

## Strategic frame

**The market**: There are ~50–80 Victorian schools racing HPR competitively. Mix of:
- **Top tier private schools** (15–20): Caulfield, Wesley, Scotch, MLC, Carey, Geelong Grammar, Haileybury. Premium budgets, specialist HPR coaches on staff, parent expectation of sport-tech.
- **Mid-tier private + select-entry public** (20–30): Mac.Robertson, Melbourne High, Suzanne Cory, Camberwell GS. Smaller budgets but technically sharp coaches.
- **Public schools with HPR programs** (15–25): Funded by school sport budget + state HPV body. Tight budgets, generalist PE teachers running the program alongside other duties.

**The wedge**: CGS-style premium edition. Charge nothing or very little to top private schools in year one to lock in adoption + endorsements. Once the marquee schools are using TurboPrep on race day with the team kit, every other school sees it on the feed at Maryborough / Wonthaggi / Casey Fields.

**The hook**: Live race-day leaderboard. It's the single feature parents, opposing coaches, and other schools' kids see — and it pulls every team in the pit lane into the app whether they signed up or not. You scan the QR on the leaderboard to watch your team's stints.

## Three-phase rollout (18 months)

### Phase 1 — Lighthouse (Months 1–4)

**Goal**: One marquee school using TurboPrep daily, on race day, with their brand on it.

**Target**: Caulfield Grammar HPR program.

**Why CGS**: Premium private, name recognition, multi-year HPR pedigree, performance-oriented program, parents who'll talk to other parents.

**Tactics**:
- Deploy the **CGS premium edition** (gold-on-white, "CGS SPORT" branding) free for the season
- One named contact: the HPR head coach (not the PE department, not the head of sport — the actual person running the program)
- Onboard the squad week 1, get 90%+ adherence by week 3
- Race day mode at the first state-round race — every CGS stint logs through the app
- Excel race export to the head of sport and the head of school — make it a visible artefact
- Photo + video the race weekend, with consent — content for partnerships

**Success metrics by end of Phase 1**:
- 100% of CGS HPR squad installed
- 80%+ weekly adherence (median)
- All race-day stints logged through TurboPrep
- One published case study (text + photos)
- Head of school + sport coordinator demo'd
- 3+ inbound requests from other schools who saw CGS using it on race day

---

### Phase 2 — Private school cluster (Months 5–10)

**Goal**: 8–12 private schools using TurboPrep, with at least 3 paying.

**Targets** (in order):
1. Wesley College — direct competitor to CGS, will move on signal
2. Scotch College — joint AGSV / APS overlap, similar program structure
3. MLC, Carey, Camberwell GS — Caulfield-adjacent schools
4. Geelong Grammar, Haileybury — boarding schools with strong sport budgets
5. Mentone, Brighton GS, Trinity — secondary private cluster

**Tactics**:
- **Inbound from Phase 1**: CGS race-day appearances drive curiosity. Pre-build a 15-min "how to evaluate TurboPrep for your team" web page with a Calendly link to a demo.
- **Sport directors not PE teachers**: target each school's *head of sport* or *director of HPR* directly. Skip the PE department — they don't make spend decisions and don't run elite HPR programs.
- **Pricing**: $0 for the rest of the season (introductory) or $800/yr per team thereafter. Negotiate up to $2,000/yr for "premium edition" custom branding (white + school colours + crest).
- **Co-marketing**: each premium-edition school becomes a case study + gets credit in the app's about page.
- **Race-day catalyst**: at every state round, hand-deliver a printed leaderboard at the end of the day to the *coach* of the top 3 teams, with a QR to a 1-pager about TurboPrep. The leaderboard alone is the demo.

**Success metrics by end of Phase 2**:
- 10+ schools onboarded
- 3+ paying customers (premium edition or annual fee)
- $5k+ ARR
- 200+ active student-athletes across all teams
- 2 named partnerships with HPV body / governing body

---

### Phase 3 — Public school + governance push (Months 11–18)

**Goal**: TurboPrep is the de-facto race-day app for Victorian HPR. Adoption hits 30+ schools.

**Targets**: Select-entry public schools, regional Catholic schools, smaller country HPR programs.

**Tactics**:
- **Partnership with Energy Breakthrough / HPV Vic**: pitch the app as official race-day infrastructure. Provide free race-leaderboard kiosks (web URL on iPad) at every state-series venue.
- **State sport peak body deal**: pitch School Sport Victoria. Position as "we already work with the top private schools — here's a tier for public + Catholic at $200/yr per team."
- **Educator endorsements**: get one high-profile HPR coach (university-level or national rep) to advocate the app publicly. Their video testimonial is the unlock for public-school principals who need a third-party signal.
- **Free tier**: Always-free tier with all core features (training plans, logging, team chat, basic leaderboard). Premium adds: race archive export, live race-day mode, custom branding, AI coach v2.
- **Multi-school showcase**: at the Maryborough state finals, run a custom leaderboard showing every TurboPrep-enabled team. Photograph it. Use it in every pitch deck for the next year.

**Success metrics by end of Phase 3**:
- 30+ schools onboarded
- 10+ paying customers
- $25k+ ARR
- 800+ active student-athletes
- Official partner status with at least one peak body (HPV Vic, School Sport Vic, or AGSV/APS)

## Target audience (refined — NOT PE teachers)

The single biggest framing mistake would be marketing this to PE departments. TurboPrep is for performance HPR programs and the athletes inside them. The decision-makers are:

### The student-athlete
The actual end user. Age 13–18. Already on TikTok, Strava, AllTrails, Garmin Connect. Knows what a good training app looks like. Will adopt or ignore TurboPrep based on:
- Whether their teammates are on it (network effect)
- Whether it feels premium (UI quality, no rough edges)
- Whether race day is genuinely better (live leaderboard, watch sync, stint replay)
- Whether the coach actually uses the data (otherwise it feels performative)

### The specialist HPR coach
Often an ex-rider, volunteer or part-time, deeply invested in the program. Knows training periodisation cold. Wants:
- Less admin (no spreadsheets)
- Better visibility into who's training and who isn't
- Tooling that helps them coach 30 kids at once
- A defensible artefact (race-day data, Excel exports) to show parents + admins

### The head of sport / sport director
The actual budget holder at the private-school level. Doesn't run programs day-to-day but decides what gets funded. Wants:
- Visible outcomes (race results, parent testimonials)
- Risk reduction (no embarrassments, no data leaks)
- School-brand alignment (this is why the premium edition matters)

### The parent
Invisible influencer. Doesn't use the app directly but hears about it from their kid + sees race-day output. If they like what they see (clear data, kids engaged, professional vibe), they advocate for renewal + recommend it at sport parent committee meetings.

**Who's NOT the target**:
- General PE teachers (don't run elite HPR programs, don't have budgets)
- Heads of school (too high-level — they delegate to head of sport)
- Junior athletes (Y7–8) on their own without an established program

The marketing copy, screenshots, and outbound pitches should reflect this. Lead with **"performance race-day platform"**, not "school PE app".

## Channel strategy

### What works

**Live race-day demos**. Every state-round race is a free trade show. Set up a leaderboard kiosk in pit lane. Walk coaches through it. Hand them a card.

**Direct outreach to head of sport**. LinkedIn + warm intro via parents/board members > generic email blast. Hit 20 schools personally over Months 5–10.

**Case studies + photos**. CGS-branded screenshots, race-day footage, athlete quotes. Build a press kit.

**Partner with governing body**. HPV Victoria, School Sport Victoria, AGSV. Get a logo on the homepage and a paragraph on theirs.

**Referral incentive**. School-to-school referrals get $200 credit. Coach-to-coach is the highest-trust signal.

### What won't work

- Cold email to generic PE department addresses
- Facebook / Instagram paid ads (audience is wrong)
- App Store ASO at scale (too niche)
- Generic "schools" PR (we're an HPR-specific tool)
- Discounting too early — premium positioning matters more than $0 revenue in Year 1

## Risks + how to handle them

**Risk 1: One school's data leak destroys trust across the network**
- Mitigation: Firestore security rules audit before every new school onboards. No PII in race-archive Excel exports. Privacy policy specific to school sport.

**Risk 2: A competitor (TrainingPeaks, Strava for Schools) launches an HPR module**
- Mitigation: HPR-specific lock-in via stint tracking + race-day mode (no general fitness app does this well). Move fast in Year 1 to make TurboPrep the default brand for "HPR app".

**Risk 3: Coach hates the UI on day 1 and bins it**
- Mitigation: The CGS lighthouse deployment isn't just for branding — it's the QA cycle that catches every UX bug before it hits a second school.

**Risk 4: HPR participation declines (declining school sport budgets)**
- Mitigation: Diversify into general school cycling clubs + adventure sports clubs in Year 3. The training + race-day stack is generalisable.

**Risk 5: Founder bandwidth — running a launch and being the only engineer**
- Mitigation: After 3 paying customers, hire a part-time engineer ($30-40k pa, fractional). Year 2 hire a launch lead at one of the partner schools.

## What happens in the first 100 days

**Days 1–14**: CGS HPR squad onboarded. Coach onboarding modal walks the head coach through subteams, invite link, weekly intent. Every athlete installed + connected.

**Days 15–30**: First training cycle complete. Coach using the dashboard 5x/week. Adherence sitting at 70%+. Weekly chat messages from coach to team are happening.

**Days 31–60**: First race day. CGS in race-day mode. Leaderboard active. Stints archived. Parents see the data. Excel export to head of school.

**Days 61–80**: Case study published. Outbound to top 8 private schools begins. Demo calls booked. Pricing conversations start.

**Days 81–100**: 2–3 new schools onboarded under "introductory" pricing. First paid customer signed. Press kit published.

By day 100, the question stops being "will this work?" and starts being "how fast can we onboard the next 10?"
