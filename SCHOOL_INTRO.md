# TurboPrep — School introduction strategy

A practical playbook for getting TurboPrep into the hands of a Victorian high-school HPR program, with zero-friction onboarding, sustainable engagement, and outcomes that defend the program to its sport director and parent community.

## Who this is for (NOT classroom PE teachers)

The target audience is the elite/competitive HPR program inside a school, not the general PE department. The decision-makers and primary users are, in order of priority:

1. **The student-athlete** (Y9–Y12, performance-oriented). The end user. They adopt or ignore TurboPrep based on whether their teammates are using it + whether the coach actually looks at the data.
2. **The specialist HPR coach** (often ex-rider, part-time, deeply technical). Wants less admin and more visibility. Lives inside the Coach dashboard.
3. **The head of sport / sport coordinator** (decision-maker on budget + program priority). Wants race-day outcomes, parent satisfaction, and a defensible artefact.
4. **The parent**. Invisible influencer. Sees race-day data + their kid's engagement level — advocates for renewal at parent committee meetings.

This document is written so a *head of sport* or *specialist HPR coach* can read it once and run the playbook. PE teachers who happen to also coach HPR are welcome, but they're not the centre of gravity.

Goal: from cold install to first race-week, every athlete logging 80%+ of prescribed sessions, with the coach in command of the data and the program visibly performant to parents + admins.

---

## Phase 0 — Coach pre-flight (15 minutes, day before launch)

Before any students touch the app, the coach needs to be set up.

1. **Install TurboPrep** on their personal iPhone via the TestFlight link `https://testflight.apple.com/join/REPLACE-ME` (or the App Store once 1.0.0 is live).
2. **Sign up as "Coach"** — pick their school as the club name, enter their full name.
3. **Wait for approval** — the head admin (you) gets a request in the Admin tab. Approve it before continuing.
4. **Create the team** — Coach tab → Manage → New Team. Use the school name (`St Bedes HPR`, `Caulfield HPV`, etc).
5. **Set subteams** — create one subteam per year level the coach is responsible for. Y9, Y10, Y11 if mixed. Subteams scope chat + adherence views, so don't skip this.
6. **Open the invite link** — Coach tab → Manage → Team → **Copy invite link**. The link looks like `https://turboprep.app/join/AB1234`. Save it.
7. **Set the weekly intent** — Coach tab → Today's hub → "Set this week's focus". One sentence the team sees every time they open the app, e.g. *"Base aerobic week — don't push the threshold sessions, log your RPE"*.

## Phase 1 — Day-1 rollout (single PE lesson, ~30 min)

The first session in front of the kids. Best done in a computer lab or with iPads, but a phone-only roll-out works fine.

### Coach script — slides text (paste into a deck)

**Slide 1 — "What is this?"**
> TurboPrep is the app we'll use this season to track training, race-day stints, and stay in touch with each other.
>
> Three things you'll do in it:
> 1. **Log every workout** — bike, gym, floor — so I can see your prep.
> 2. **Tap a plan** — your training plan is already in here; you just open and tick.
> 3. **Race day** — your stints, lap times, and team leaderboard all live in this one app.

**Slide 2 — "Why now?"**
> The state series is `<insert date>` weeks away. The team that arrives logged, healthy, and on the same page wins.
>
> No app = no data = guessing. Guessing doesn't win races.

**Slide 3 — "How to join"** (have this on the projector with QR + link)
> 1. Open the camera on your phone → scan this QR.
>    `<QR code from coach invite link>`
> 2. Tap "Install" if it's the App Store, or "Accept" → "Install" in TestFlight.
> 3. Open the app → tap **"Join an existing team"**.
> 4. The team code `AB1234` is already pre-filled if you scanned the QR.
> 5. Pick your year level + tier (Basic / Average / Intense — I'll tell you which).
> 6. Hit **"Join"**. You should see my name as your coach.

### Coach hands-on (during the lesson)

While athletes are signing up:
- Walk the room, help anyone stuck on Apple ID / email password / TestFlight install.
- For each athlete who appears in the roster — quickly tap their card → assign their plan if it isn't already.
- Set up the team's first chat message: pin a welcome note in team chat with the season calendar.

### Common day-1 frictions + fixes

| Problem | Fix |
|---|---|
| "I don't have an iPhone" | The web app at `turboprep.app` works on any browser. Tell them to bookmark it. No watch features, but everything else works. |
| Apple ID password forgotten | Skip them for now; have them sort it at lunch. |
| TestFlight "no more spots" | Move 5 athletes to the App Store path if 1.0.0 is live, or expand TestFlight slots in App Store Connect. |
| Year level / tier wrong | Tap the athlete card → "Manage" → re-assign. |
| "I can't see the team" | They probably skipped the team code. Settings → Switch team → enter the code. |

## Phase 2 — Week 1 (the engagement curve)

The first week is where 80% of teams fail. Athletes log once on day 1, then drift. To prevent that:

### Coach actions

**Day 2 (the morning after):**
- Open the Coach tab. Anyone with **0 sessions** logged gets a tap → "Send chat message" → quick personal nudge.
- Don't shame; ask: *"Hey Sam, didn't see anything logged from yesterday — did you train? Drop it in even if it was a 20-min spin."*

**Day 4 (mid-week):**
- Check the **adherence %** column. Anyone under 40% gets a 1:1 message + a quick chat in PE.
- Anyone over 80% gets a shoutout in team chat (tap the 👏 button). Public praise drives the rest of the team.

**Day 7 (end of week 1):**
- Set next week's intent in the coach hub.
- Post one team-wide chat message summarising: "Top loggers this week: X, Y, Z. Sessions completed: N/M. Next week we're focusing on [specific phase task]."

### Athlete behaviour patterns

| Pattern | What to do |
|---|---|
| Logs everything on day 1, then disappears | Send a personal message Day 3. Don't wait. |
| Only logs the easy sessions | Ask about the hard ones — they may be embarrassed about RPE. Reframe: "Logging the bad days helps me coach you, not judge you." |
| Logs but uses RPE 5 for everything | Add a coach note in chat explaining the scale (3 = easy chat, 7 = uncomfortable, 9 = max). |
| Doesn't log but trains | Check in person. They may not understand the value. One demo of the Coach dashboard usually fixes it. |
| Logs phantom workouts | Rare but real. Confirm via chat. If clearly fake, ask them to retract. Don't make it a public thing. |

## Phase 3 — Week 2-4 (consolidation)

Goal: 80% team adherence and the coach using the dashboard daily.

**Coach daily routine (5 min):**
1. Open Coach tab → Students.
2. Sort by adherence %. Look at the bottom 5.
3. Send a 1:1 nudge to anyone under 50%.
4. Glance at the top 5 — drop a 🔥 in chat.
5. Check team chat for unanswered questions.

**Weekly routine (15 min, Sunday night):**
1. Coach tab → My Team → review the week's stats.
2. Set next week's intent.
3. Plan any in-PE-class session (use the "PE-friendly" filter in the plans library — sessions that fit a 40-min period).
4. Message the team with the week ahead.

**Plan adjustments to make:**
- After 4 weeks, **switch all athletes to the next phase** of their plan (Base → Build, Build → Peak). The plan picker in their Today tab will suggest it.
- Anyone over 90% adherence and feeling great → bump them to a higher tier (basic → average, average → intense).
- Anyone struggling at <40% → drop them a tier OR have a conversation about scheduling (school workload, sleep).

## Phase 4 — Race week (the payoff)

Race-week auto-sharpening kicks in automatically when the next race is ≤7 days away. The app will:
- Compress all athletes' weekly plans to **2 short sharpener sessions + 4 rest days + 1 race day**.
- Show "RACE WEEK" in big letters at the top of every screen.
- Pin a coach checklist with race-day prep (bike check, hydration, sleep target).

**Coach actions:**
- Race day morning — toggle "Race day mode" in Coach tab → Race Day. This:
  - Pins the leaderboard to every athlete's home screen
  - Activates the Apple Watch stint-tracking lock screen
  - Auto-saves every stint to the team's race archive
- After race — within 24 h, post the race summary to chat. Tap the **"Export race day to Excel"** button to download a full stint-by-stint spreadsheet for the school office / parents.

## Phase 5 — Off-season + parent communication

**Parent comms template** — send via email at end of week 1:

> Hi {parent name},
>
> Your athlete has just signed up to TurboPrep, the training and race-day app we're using this HPR season. A few notes:
>
> 1. **What it does** — tracks training sessions, plans their week, and runs the live race-day leaderboard.
> 2. **Privacy** — we collect: workout logs, RPE (effort), heart rate (only if they wear an Apple Watch), and chat in the team feed. Nothing is shared outside the team. Data is hosted by Firebase (Google) and Australia-based servers. Full privacy policy: `https://turboprep.app/privacy`.
> 3. **Screen time** — the app is designed for one quick log per training day (under 2 min). Excessive scrolling isn't required.
> 4. **What you can do** — ask your athlete to show you their week. If they're not logging, that's a conversation worth having.
>
> Questions? Hit reply.
>
> — {coach name}

## Phase 6 — Coach calibration scorecard

After 4 weeks, score the rollout honestly:

| Metric | Target | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| % of team installed | 95%+ | One non-installer corrupts team chat + leaderboard data |
| 7-day median adherence | 70%+ | Below this, the data is too thin to coach from |
| Number of team chat messages this week | 5+ | Cultural signal — silent chat = silent team |
| Athletes who logged last 2 weeks consecutively | 60%+ | Habit formation threshold |
| Race-day stints saved last race | 100% of riders | If anyone's missing, the data is incomplete |

If any line scores red, that's the focus for the next 2 weeks.

## Phase 7 — Common failure modes (and how to avoid them)

**Failure 1: "Just another app the school made us download"**
- Fix: tie it to a visible benefit on day 1. Show the coach dashboard *with their data* by day 4 — "Here's exactly what you logged, and what it tells me."

**Failure 2: Coach stops opening the dashboard**
- Fix: schedule a 5-min daily review into the coach's morning routine. Put it on their calendar.

**Failure 3: Adherence drops in week 3 (post-novelty)**
- Fix: introduce a "training streak" leaderboard at end of week 2. The 🔥 chip already exists in the app — point everyone at it in team chat.

**Failure 4: One year level adopts, the other doesn't**
- Fix: subteam scoping was probably missed. Set up subteams properly, then have the lagging year's lead coach personally onboard each kid.

**Failure 5: Privacy / parent push-back**
- Fix: parent comms template above sent BEFORE day 1. Pre-emptive, not reactive.

**Failure 6: The app breaks during race day**
- Fix: the dry-run. Two weeks before any real race, do a 30-min mock stint in the school car park. Every athlete starts/ends a stint. You'll catch any device-specific bugs.

## Phase 8 — When to expand

After a successful first season:
1. **Add a second year level** — same playbook, week 1 onboarding.
2. **Pull in assistant coaches** — Coach tab → Manage → Co-Coach. They get read access to the dashboard.
3. **Sister schools** — if you're running an inter-school invitational, every team gets its own TurboPrep team + invite link; race day mode handles cross-team leaderboards automatically.
4. **Annual archive** — at end of season, use the Excel export to create a "Season Book" the team takes to its end-of-year dinner.
